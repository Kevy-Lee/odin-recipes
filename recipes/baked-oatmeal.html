Blueberry Baked Oats

Home

Description

Start your day with a warm and wholesome treat: blueberry baked oats! This comforting recipe combines the natural sweetness of ripe blueberries with the hearty goodness of rolled oats, creating a breakfast that feels indulgent yet nourishing. Ideal for busy mornings, it can be prepped ahead of time and easily reheated, making it a convenient option for meal preppers or anyone looking to simplify their routine. Whether you enjoy it plain or topped with a drizzle of maple syrup or a dollop of yogurt, this dish is a satisfying way to fuel your day.

The beauty of blueberry baked oats lies in its versatility. You can customize the recipe with your favorite mix-ins, like chopped nuts, shredded coconut, or a handful of chocolate chips, to suit your cravings. It’s also adaptable for various dietary preferences—swap dairy milk for almond or oat milk, or use a flax egg to make it vegan. The baked oats develop a lightly golden crust while remaining soft and tender inside, giving you a delightful contrast of textures. With its vibrant colors and irresistible aroma, this dish isn’t just a breakfast; it’s a reason to jump out of bed in the morning!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 2 tsps cinnamon powder
  • 3/4 tsp sea salt
  • 2 tsps granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup semi-skimmed milk
  • 2 medium bananas
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tbsps melted butter
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tsps vanilla extract
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1/2 cup crushed toasted pecans

  1. Preheat the oven to 375F / 190C. Once the oven has finished preheating, pour the pecans onto a rimmed baking sheet. Toast for 4 to 5 minutes, until fragrant.
  2. Blend the rolled oats into oat flour. In a medium mixing bowl, combine the oat flour, blueberries, toasted nuts, cinnamon, baking powder, salt, and nutmeg. Whisk to combine.
  3. In a smaller mmixing bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until completely smooth. Stir the melted butter into the mashed bananas. Then, combine the milk, honey, egg, butter, and vanilla. Whisk until blended.
  4. Drizzle the wet ingredients over the dry oat mixture, whisking gently until a consistent texture is achieved. Pour the batter inito your prepared baking dish.
  5. Scatter any remaining berries across the top of the batter. Sprinkle some raw sugar on top for extra sweetness and crunch (optional).
  6. Bake for 55 to 60 minutes (if using frozen berries, add an extra 5 minutes), until the top is nice and golden. Remove your baked oats from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Drizzle remaining melted butter on the top before serving (optional).
  7. Serve as-is or with toppings of your choice. This baked oatmeal is fantastic when served warm, but is also good at room temperature or chilled. It keeps well in the refrigerator, covered, for 4 to 5 days. If desired, simply reheat individual portions in the microwave before serving. Enjoy!
